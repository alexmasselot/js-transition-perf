Experimenting transition with d3.js & pixi.js

The setup

A set of particles are launched in a box, and their positions are randomly incremented at regular time intervals. To smoothen the display, we want the particle not to jump, but to follow a continuous trajectory. Both d3.js css transitions and pixi.js mechanisms are explored (with different flavors), varying the number of particles, the time interval length to measure browser performances.

This page sustains the blog post QQQ

How to use?

Head for the menu bar and hack the parameters to change the behavior:
  • n=integer: the number of particles
  • refresh=integer: the time interval (in milliseconds)
  • leap=float in ]0,0.5]: the average distance for the next particle position (relative to the screen width)
